# ResearchQ v0.0.1 — Initial Release

**Turn months of manual literature review into days of structured, reproducible, AI-assisted research.**

ResearchQ is a purpose-built platform for systematic reviews, scoping reviews, and evidence syntheses — combining the OpenAlex academic graph (250M+ papers) with Google Gemini to automate discovery, screening, extraction, and synthesis while keeping you in control of every decision.

## What's Included

- **AI-configured projects** — describe your domain in plain language; ResearchQ generates your keywords, analysis dimensions, and relevance criteria (all editable).
- **Six search strategies** — Journal, Author, Topic, Database (IEEE, ACM, Springer, Elsevier, Wiley, SAGE…), Cited By, and References — every search logged and resumable.
- **AI relevance screening** — every paper evaluated against *your* project's criteria, not a generic classifier.
- **Automatic metadata extraction** — summary, methodology, population, region, platform, keywords.
- **Cross-reference matrix** — sortable, filterable comparison table; export to CSV, BibTeX, or JSON.
- **18 built-in methodologies** — PRISMA, Cochrane, Kitchenham SLR, PRISMA-ScR, Arksey & O'Malley, JBI, PICO/SPIDER, ENTREQ, Meta-ethnography, GRADE, CASP, Newcastle-Ottawa, and more.
- **Guided phase execution** with **20 auto-generated outputs** — PRISMA flow diagrams, inclusion/exclusion tables, data extraction matrices, bibliometric analyses, thematic syntheses, protocol documents, GRADE evidence tables — downloadable as Markdown or DOCX.
- **Reproducibility by default** — every search, screening decision, and extraction is recorded.

## Built For

PhD students starting their first systematic review · postdocs and faculty juggling multiple review projects · research labs collaborating on shared reviews · interdisciplinary teams · grant applicants needing rapid literature landscapes.
